## Featured: Multi-Team SOP

**[Managed Endpoint Onboarding — New Client Site →](showcase/endpoint-onboarding-sop.html){target=_blank}**

A cross-team Standard Operating Procedure that coordinates three teams — Provisioning, Security Operations, and Service Desk — through a sequenced onboarding with explicit handoffs.

What this document demonstrates:

- **Per-step ownership metadata** — every step carries a *Responsible role*, *Expected Time*, and *Success Criteria*, because in a multi-team procedure each handoff needs an unambiguous owner and a defined completion gate.
- **A visual workflow grid** that maps the five phases to their owning teams at a glance.
- **Sequencing controls** — callouts that enforce order across teams (Security can't begin until Provisioning confirms check-in).
- **Classification and governance** — TLP:AMBER metadata, a quick-answer summary, prerequisites, a troubleshooting matrix, ISO/IEC 27001 control mappings, version history, and a controlled-document footer.

Open it in a new tab to see the full styling render live.

## Two SOP patterns

I design SOPs in two patterns depending on accountability:

**Single-owner SOP** — linear steps performed by one team. Clean and fast; no per-step ownership needed because one role owns the whole procedure.

**Multi-team SOP** (featured above) — adds per-step *Responsible / Expected Time / Success Criteria* metadata, because work crosses team boundaries and each handoff must be explicit. The added structure isn't decoration; it's what prevents a cross-team procedure from stalling at an ambiguous handoff.

Choosing the right pattern for the situation is part of the craft — more structure is not always better, only when accountability is distributed.
