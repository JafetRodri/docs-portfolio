Executive Summary

This policy defines how Professional Services teams govern client engagements: who can approve work, when to escalate, how to handle exceptions, and how to close an engagement. The objective is that every engagement follows the same controls, so quality and risk are managed consistently.

1. Policy Statement

1.1 Purpose

This policy establishes clear, uniform rules for engagement approval, escalation, exception handling, and closure across all Professional Services work.

1.2 Scope

Applies to:

  • Professional Services team members delivering scoped work.
  • Project and engagement managers.
  • Security and operations teams supporting key decisions.
  • Client contacts with approval or acceptance authority.

Out of scope: legal terms defined in the Master Services Agreement, and routine managed-services work outside an approved engagement.

3. Mandatory Governance Requirements

Key Requirement

Every engagement must apply four control categories: approval authority, escalation, exception management, and closure control.

3.1 Approval Authority

  1. Each engagement names a primary and backup approval role at kickoff.
  2. Scope, timeline, and major risk decisions require written approval.
  3. If authority is unclear, escalate to Professional Services leadership before deciding.

3.2 Escalation

Escalate immediately when a security risk needs containment, a critical service issue occurs, a schedule slip exceeds approved limits, or a blocked sign-off prevents progress. Each escalation records the date, owner, action taken, and outcome, and is reviewed until closed.

3.3 Exception Management

  1. Each exception records the business reason, risk, compensating controls, owner, and end date.
  2. Exceptions are reviewed at least every 30 days; security exceptions require security review.
Compliance Risk

An expired exception that is not renewed is not neutral — it becomes a non-compliance condition and must be escalated immediately.

3.4 Closure Control

Closure requires a package covering deliverable status, open risks, handoff records, and recorded client acceptance. Closure approval is recorded by the designated approval role; if criteria are unmet, the engagement stays open with corrective actions assigned.

6. Compliance and Enforcement

Compliance Snapshot

All non-compliance is recorded and escalated to leadership. Repeated control failures require a remediation plan. Severe or security-impacting non-compliance is escalated immediately to leadership and security.
