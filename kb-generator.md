# Quick Start: Turn On Two-Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second layer of security to your account. Even if someone learns your password, they can't sign in without the code from your phone. This guide takes about **five minutes**.

!!! note "About this sample"
    This is a customer-facing quick-start guide written for a non-technical audience. It uses a generic account-security scenario to demonstrate friendly, task-focused onboarding documentation.

## Before you start

You'll need:

- Your account email and password
- A smartphone with an authenticator app installed (such as Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator)

Don't have an authenticator app yet? Install one from your phone's app store before continuing — it's free.

## Step 1 — Open your security settings

1. Sign in to your account.
2. Click your profile picture in the top-right corner.
3. Select **Settings**, then **Security**.

You'll see a section labeled **Two-Factor Authentication**.

## Step 2 — Start setup

Click **Turn on 2FA**. A window appears showing a **QR code** — a square black-and-white pattern.

Keep this window open. You'll scan the code in the next step.

## Step 3 — Scan the code with your app

1. Open your authenticator app.
2. Tap the **+** button to add a new account.
3. Choose **Scan a QR code**.
4. Point your phone's camera at the QR code on your screen.

Your app instantly adds the account and begins showing a **6-digit code** that refreshes every 30 seconds.

## Step 4 — Confirm it works

1. Back in your browser, type the current 6-digit code from your app into the confirmation box.
2. Click **Verify**.

If the code is correct, you'll see a green confirmation message. 2FA is now active. :material-check-circle:

## Step 5 — Save your backup codes

After setup, you'll be given a list of **backup codes**. These let you sign in if you ever lose your phone.

!!! warning "Don't skip this"
    Save your backup codes somewhere safe — a password manager is ideal. Without them, losing your phone can lock you out of your account.

## You're done

The next time you sign in, you'll enter your password as usual, then the 6-digit code from your app. That's it — your account is now significantly harder to break into.

---

**Having trouble?** If the code is rejected, check that your phone's clock is set to update automatically. Authenticator codes are time-based, and a clock that's even a minute off can cause codes to fail.
